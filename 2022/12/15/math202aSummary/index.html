Def[Hausdorff space]: A topological space (X,T)(X,\mathcal{T})(X,T) is Hausdorff iff. for any two different points x,y∈Xx,y\in Xx,y∈X, we can find disjoint open neighborhoods of them.

Def[Normal space]: A topological space (X,T)(X,\mathcal{T})(X,T) is normal iff. for any disjoint closed sets E,F⊂XE,F\subset XE,F⊂X, we can find disjoint open neighborhoods of them.

Comparing convergence

  1. For simple integrable functions, Cauchy in measure is equivalent with mean Cauchy.
  2. For measurable functions, almost uniform convergence implies convergence in measure.
  3. For measurable functions, almost uniform Cauchy implies Cauchy in measure.
  4. For any sequence of functions that converges almost uniformly, it converges almost everywhere.
