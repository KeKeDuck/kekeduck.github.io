Here I share some math resources for autodidacts.

YouTube channels:

  • 3Blue1Brown. My favorite.
  • Michael Penn. There is a trade-off between rigor and intuition when teaching math. This channel does not have incredible visualization/animation like 3Blue1Brown. But you can find more details and proofs here.
  • MIT OpenCourseWare.
  • Oxford Mathematics. You can find undergraduate open courses here. Their playlists did not take in all their videos. Use the search botton in the channel to find out more.
  • Institute for Advanced Study. Math frontiers.
  • Fields Institute. Math frontiers.
  • Instituto de Matemática Pura e Aplicada. Math frontiers.
  • Institut des Hautes Études Scientifiques (IHÉS). Math frontiers.
  • Institute for Pure & Applied Mathematics (IPAM). Math frontiers.
  • The Bright Side of Mathematics. You can find nice playlists that cover math undergrad course contents, including Real Analysis, Linear Algebra, measure theory, functional analysis.
  • Dr Peyam. Nice examples and problem solving processes.
  • sudgylacmoe. I like the videos on Geometric algebra (Clifford algebra).
  • ThatMathThing
  • John Cagnol. The PDE videos are nice.
  • Virtually Passed. Nice visualization.
  • Numberphile. The channel invited many mathematicians to deliver small talks on interesting topics.
  • Steve Brunton. Applied math with engineering flavor.
  • eigenchris. I learned a lot from its videos on relativity and differential geometry.
  • Mutual Information. Information theory and statistics.
  • MetaMaths. Interesting topics.
  • Dialect. I like its videos on metric tensor.
  • Nathaniel Johnston. Nice linear algebra courses.
  • 臺大開放式課程 NTU OCW. Nice courses in Chinese.
  • 東大TV / UTokyo TV.
  • Aleph 0. I like its videos on Stokes’ theorem and derivative.
  • Petra Bonfert-Taylor. Nice lecture videos on complex analysis.

Visualization of engineering methods:
